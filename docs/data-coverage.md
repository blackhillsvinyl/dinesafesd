# Data coverage and known gaps

What DineSafeSD covers, where the data comes from, and the gaps we know about.
Last reviewed: 2026-07.

## Sources

| Source | Coverage | Cadence | What it provides |
|---|---|---|---|
| SD DOH public inspection portal (`sddoh.safefoodinspection.com`) | All 66 counties, Food classification | Synced daily; full re-crawl monthly | Establishment name/address/phone, latest inspection (date, score, violations with citation text), past inspection dates + violation counts, report PDFs |
| Sioux Falls SWEEPS (`sweepsdata.siouxfalls.gov`) | City of Sioux Falls | Synced daily | Name/address/phone, scores, full violation details for each establishment's **two most recent** inspections |

## Known gaps

### Tribal lands
Food service on reservations is generally licensed and inspected by the
Indian Health Service or tribal health authorities, not SD DOH. Those
inspections are not published in either source above, so reservation
communities (e.g. Pine Ridge, Rosebud, Cheyenne River) are under-represented.
No public IHS inspection feed is known; revisit if one appears.

### Historical score depth
The state only posts the **last 2 years** of scores — each new score replaces
the oldest, so our store is the durable record and captured data is not
recoverable from the source later. The portal's "Past Inspections" expander
lists dates and violation counts but **not scores** — scores exist only
inside each inspection's report PDF. The `backfill:scores` job downloads and parses those PDFs to fill
history (see `services/data-pipeline/src/jobs/backfill-scores.ts`). PDFs are not
available for every old inspection; where missing, the inspection appears
with a violation count but no score, and trend lines fall back to violation
counts.

### Sioux Falls two-inspection window
SWEEPS publishes itemized violations only for the last two inspections per
establishment. We retain everything we've captured since syncing began, so
our history grows over time, but pre-2025 Sioux Falls detail is limited to
what the city still displays.

### Scope
Food-service licenses only. Lodging, pools, tattoo, and other DOH-inspected
license types are intentionally out of scope.

### Geocoding precision
Every restaurant carries a `geo_precision` flag (`rooftop`, `address`,
`street`, `city`). `city`-level entries render with an "approximate
location" note in the UI. See `docs/address-audit.md` (generated by the
regeocode migration) for the current tier breakdown.
